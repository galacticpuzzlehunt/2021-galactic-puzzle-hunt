Solution Combat Roller
Answer MORTAL KOMBAT ARMAGEDDON

Written by Yannick Yao

We are presented with an interface that allows us to “craft” fighters and have them fight against each other. Experimenting with Practice Mode reveals some constraints on the fighters:

  • They must have exactly 6 letters (otherwise the interface will complain that “the fighter is not fit for combat”); and
  • They must be a valid Scrabble word.

These constraints are subtly clued by the title, which consists of two six-letter words. Once the conditions are satisfied, we may now have the words fight each other. The “pre-fight analysis” shows the win probability for each fighter, and then a fight occurs, with the result determined by these probabilities. Once we collect enough examples, we may note that:

  • The probabilities are symmetric with respect to the fighter positions (i.e. swapping fighter 1 and 2 also swaps the win probabilities);
  • Words with more letters that appear later in the alphabet are more likely to win, especially if those letters are towards the end of the word;
  • Pairs of identical words or very similar words have a high draw probability (at least 20% for identical words);
  • The probabilities are always a multiple of 1/441 (the numbers are rounded to 0.001%, which is certainly enough precision for this observation).

Eventually, we can figure out how the combat actually works: each word is treated as a 6-sided die with one letter on each face, weighted in the ratio of 1:2:3:4:5:6 based on the position of the letters in the word. In each combat the two dice are rolled, and the one that showed a letter that is later in the alphabet wins.

Using these observations, we can now pick words to fight against some of the preset fighters to figure out what they are. Here are some useful strategies:

  • Use pairs of similar words (ideally differing by only one letter) and take note of the changes in probabilities.
  • Use words with few unique letters and observe the draw probabilities to rule out possible letters or to determine which letter appears in the word.

The preset fighters are BARAKA, HOTARU, KENSHI, and MAVADO, which turned out to be all fighters from Mortal Kombat series.

We can now also pick a word to enter the Campaign Mode and try to figure out what the opponents are, but the main difference is that you can only enter the Campaign once (per reset), and you cannot see your results against the later words unless you beat the previous words. Hence, it is helpful to pick strong words (such as SYZYGY) to make sure that you get the most out of each run.

There are five stages, each with different opponent: SERIES, NUMBER, SEVENS, TITLED, ANSWER. This is a cluephrase indicating that the answer is the title of the seventh (main) game of the Mortal Kombat series. Moreover, once you beat all five opponents, a message says “Congratulations, you cleared Campaign Mode! Your prize is: ___ ___ ___”, indicating that the “prize” (i.e. the answer) consists of three words. Therefore, the answer is MORTAL KOMBAT ARMAGEDDON.

Author’s Notes

The original idea was to make a puzzle about intransitive dice, but nothing concrete came up, so I turned it into a black-box interactive puzzle instead. (Nothing can go wrong with a black box, right?)

To make Campaign Mode a bit less luck-dependent, each round is in fact a first-to-3 series, so if you have a good word you will get better odds than what the probabilities suggest.
