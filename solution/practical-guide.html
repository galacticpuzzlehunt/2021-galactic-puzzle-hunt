Solution A Practical Guide to Choosing a Team Name
Answer STATE MACHINE

Written by Alan Huang

As we begin trying to solve the clues, we may notice some patterns. It turns out that each team has a particular naming scheme: its clue hints at a phrase made of two words that are anagrams of each other. Each team then goes on to participate in one of the hunts using an appropriate name: another anagram of its favored set of letters, embedded within a phrase that’s related to the hunt theme. Once we realize this, we can work back and forth, using both sets of clues to narrow down the possibilities.

The third anagram always spans the word boundary, and if it’s removed from the phrase, the remaining letters look almost like words. There’s a unique way to replace the anagram with a single letter to form a common English word. The hunt clues are given in alphabetical order; reading the extra letters in order by team clue gives the answer STATE MACHINE.
