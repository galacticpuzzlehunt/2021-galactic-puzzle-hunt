Solution All Things Big and Small
Answer EMBELLISHING

Written by Brian Chen

The puzzle consists of a bunch of overlaid clues in tiny font with all text information removed. To figure out what the clues say, solvers can zoom in a lot on the webpage or the PDF, possibly with the help of an editor, or even find the matching font (Fira Sans Thin) and superimpose letters.

Each clue is a wordplay-only cryptic clue that solves to the name of a traditional point size minus a letter. Each clue is also written in a point size, and the clues are also vertically sorted by point size to help solvers visually distinguish them; we can arrange the clues in a cycle such that each clue’s answer is the next clue’s point size minus a letter.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle largely wrote itself after I accidentally stumbled upon the Wikipedia article Traditional point-size names.

I have thought about puzzle ideas that involve simple visual manipulation of English letters for a while, e.g. puzzles like Bottom Lines, as they seem like they should be an area of the most accessible puzzle ideas in terms of knowledge, while there’s still a lot to explore. I think I’d enjoy annotating the strokes, analyzing baselines and spacing, etc. to tease apart the letters in this puzzle; but many of my testsolvers didn’t and I ran out of time/ideas for making it better, so I put it last in the round so that more teams would be able to backsolve it if they so wanted — with, apparently, limited success.

I maybe use Fira Sans too much, but there aren’t that many options for fonts with thin enough weights to be overlaid as in the puzzle and still have discernable strokes. I thought features like Fira’s slightly curved lowercase L would make it easier.
