Solution Everything Everywhere All at Once
Answer BUILDING CONSTRUCTION

Written by Bex Lin and Anderson Wang

The puzzle features three word banks, each with 15 items: Everything (in Spring), a set of objects; Everywhere (in Summer), a set of locations (each with numbers attached); and All at Once (in Fall), a set of time periods / events (again, each with numbers attached). Then, finally, there is a set of descriptions (in Winter), each one missing three blanks.

Each description can be matched with an object from Everything, a location from Everywhere, and a time from All at Once. The three blanks all correspond to details from a movie and “Evelyn” corresponds to a character from the movie, but one sentence actually refers to something (typically a superpower) associated with a superhero (or villain) character played by the same actor. Note that, in this ordering, the actors are also in order by last name, which can help with confirmation.

The superheroes are separated into two columns: for the superheroes in the left column, the sentence from the description that references the superhero also contains the blank from Everywhere; for the superheroes in the right column, the sentence that references them contains the blank from All at Once. For example, Batman is referenced in a sentence with “Iran (4/6)” from Everywhere, placing him in the left column.

We can also notice that the second of the two numbers corresponds to the length of the name of the superhero, e.g. “Batman” has length 6, which matches the 6 in “Iran (4/6)”. This should help confirm whether we’re looking for “Bruce Wayne” or “Batman”.

At this point, we have two pairs of numbers per description, with one of them matching up to a superhero. In order to use the other pair, we need one last aha: each actor has played both Marvel and DC characters (this is clued by the fact that the right and left columns of superheroes are DC and Marvel characters, respectively). In particular, each actor has played a unique character from the other comic universe, whose length matches the second number in the other pair, e.g. Ben Affleck portrayed Daredevil in the 2003 film, and ‚ÄúDaredevil‚Äù has length 9, matching the 9 in “1979 (8/9)”.

We get the cluephrases “NASHVILLE BATMAN” and “FISK UNION ALLIED”. These phrases correspond to the blanks at the bottom of the Winter page, referring to “NASHVILLE BATMAN BUILDING” (“Batman Building” being a nickname for the AT&T Building in Nashville due to its appearance) and “FISK UNION ALLIED CONSTRUCTION” (“Union Allied Construction” being a company established by Wilson Fisk in the Marvel universe).

Author’s Notes

Anderson: This puzzle spent a lot of time in brainstorming—we had a lot of ideas floating around, including trying to do things with multiverse-themed properties like Spider-man: Into the Spider-Verse or the Flashpoint storyline, but we couldn’t find enough well-known examples. Eventually, we wrote this puzzle as a mashup of 3 ideas we had (movie identification with object/place/event, characters getting “superpowers” from other characters played by the same actor, and actors in both Marvel and DC films). As a result, the puzzle might not be that cohesive, but it certainly is true to its Everything Everywhere All at Once inspiration.

Some other fun facts:

  1. We watched Sonic the Hedgehog 2 together while writing this puzzle, partly for “research”—it was pretty good. Bex also ended up watching a handful of other movies for research (and wrote all the plot descriptions), so I am very impressed with his dedication.

  2. One testsolving group found that Wikipedia said Snowpiercer took place 18 years after the catastrophe instead of 17, even though the movie explicitly states that it is 17 years. We edited Wikipedia to correct this, and I discovered that it used to say 17 until someone mysteriously changed it to 18 last year and it stuck.

  3. Somehow, this is the fourth puzzle I’ve helped write about a movie I haven’t seen (though unlike the previous puzzles, I very much do plan on eventually watching Everything Everywhere). Also, Bex has seen the movie like 6 times so it averages out, right?
