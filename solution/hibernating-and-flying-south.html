We start by noticing that the first letters of the answers in the order given spell out BANJO LEVEL. This, combined with the theming of the round as four seasonal variations, leads us to find the Click Clock Wood level of Banjo-Kazooie, which similarly has four different versions, each corresponding to a different season.

Further research shows that Click Clock Wood has 10 jiggies, a collectible represented by a gold puzzle piece. The fact that there are also 10 answers in this round suggests we wish to correspond these two sets. There are actually two such correspondences we find. From these two correspondences, we can reorder the puzzles into a cycle, starting with the one marked with a ‚òÖ. There are two possible cycle orders, but only one of them has answer lengths that make the given indices valid. Thus, we choose the order in which a puzzle’s availability indicates the next puzzle in the cycle. That is, we consider each puzzle as associated with the jiggy that matches the semantic meaning of the puzzle’s answer. The availability of that puzzle across seasons matches the availability of the jiggy associated with the next puzzle in the cycle. This is also the more natural of the two orderings, as we expect solvers to consider the puzzle answer as more of an intrinsic property of the puzzle, whereas the availability is more of an auxiliary property.

Author’s Notes

One of the initial answers for this meta was SEASONED XO. I really liked the triple meaning on “seasoned” (though I guess they all come from the same etymological root), and the double meaning of XO as the sauce and executive officer. Unfortunately, testsolving showed that the meanings of XO weren’t that widely known, and the X was too much of a single-point of failure in getting the final answer.

Another anecdote is that the factchecker for this puzzle found two alternate ways to collect jiggies that we chose not to fully reflect in this puzzle. These choices were made to prioritize solver experience (based on the sources they are likely to find and use during the hunt) over factual correctness (what is actually possible in the game). For the jiggy that is intended to be reached by leaf platforms, it is possible to drop down onto it and skip the leaves altogether. However, since getting above it in the first place would involve jumping upwards (as the jiggy only appears in seasons where flight is not available), it remained technically correct to keep the answer JUMPED UP associated with it. The second discovery was that the jiggy in Gnawty’s house can be collected in either autumn or winter, rather than only in autumn, as the puzzle implies. This could have been reflected by making the second part plus answer submission box available in winter as well as in autumn. However, given that this possibility is almost never mentioned in online sources, we ultimately decided that the inclusion of it would confuse solvers more than it would help.
