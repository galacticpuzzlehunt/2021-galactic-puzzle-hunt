Now, we have to figure out what to do with the blanks. It turns out that each of these cryptic answers can be used to find a corresponding word or phrase ending in -ology that fits in the blank. FISHES -> ICHTHYOLOGY and BLOOD -> HEMATOLOGY provide good break-ins.

However, some of these cryptic answers are pretty weird or don’t have obvious corresponding -ologies (e.g. SEWER RATS, X-RAY VISION). By Googling words in groups, we can find that these are all topics covered on Alie Ward’s podcast Ologies. All episodes on Ologies are written in the form “Exampleology (THING BEING STUDIED)”, for example “Urban Rodentology (SEWER RATS)” and “Radiology (X-RAY VISION)”.

We can now use the names of the Ologies to fill in the blanks and extract letters.

Author’s Notes

I’ve been listening to the Ologies podcast for several years. It managed to stay a part of my weekly routine even while I was struggling with mental illness, getting kicked out of MIT campus because of COVID, and transitioning to full-time employment.

I love Alie’s secrets at the end of each episode. I wanted to pay my respects to this podcast somehow with a puzzle, and the end-of-episode secrets were weird and varied enough that they just felt like good puzzle material. I decided to look up what the name of a study of secrets would be, and came out with “cryptology”. The rest of the puzzle fell into place naturally from there.

Thank you to my editors and testsolvers for helping me write cryptic clues with more interesting definitions, better surface readings, and more interesting words for extraction.
