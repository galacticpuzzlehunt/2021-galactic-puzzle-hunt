Solution A Winding Walk
Answer ASSORTED COLORS

Written by Andy Hauge

This puzzle appears in both the Summer and Autumn seasons. The flavor text changes in both, but all the main puzzle text is the same between both versions. The Autumn version also adds a diagram at the top of the puzzle. The puzzle can be solved in the Summer version, but the guidance provided by the diagram in the Autumn version makes it easier to solve.

A good starting point is to follow the link in the flavor text, which takes us to the website for a deck of cards called the Decktet. There are several noteworthy things that distinguish the Decktet from a regular deck of cards.

  • There are only thirty-six cards in the deck, compared with a standard deck‚Äôs fifty-two cards
  • All of the cards in the Decktet are identified as different characters, locations, and events.
  • The Decktet has six different suits (Moons, Suns, Waves, Leaves, Wyrms, Knots), and most cards in the Decktet have two different suits rather than one!

With this information in mind, we can start tackling the bulk of the puzzle: the jumbled daily entries. Some of the entries can be ordered by following the logic of the story, such as the entry where the pilgrim receives a strange medallion and the entry where the pilgrim shows a strange medallion off.
